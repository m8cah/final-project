Elevate your style with suits that embody sophistication and craftsmanship.

Key Characteristics of Tailored Suits

Fit and Silhouette

  • Cut: Tailored to fit the wearer's body shape closely but comfortably.
  • Silhouette: Clean lines and structured shoulders for a polished look.
  • Details: High-quality stitching and finishing for durability and refinement.

Fabric and Material

  • Materials: Wool, cotton, linen, blends.
  • Quality: Fine fabrics with a smooth finish convey luxury.
  • Patterns: Solid colors like navy, charcoal, and black; Pinstripes and subtle checks.

Versatility

  • Occasions: Formal events, business meetings, weddings.
  • Adaptability: Styled up or down with different shirts, ties, accessories.

Tailoring Details

  • Customization: Tailored or adjusted to measurements.
  • Accents: Functional buttons, lapel types (notch, peak, shawl), pocket styles (flap, jetted).

Essential Components of a Tailored Suit

Jacket

Single-breasted or double-breasted with tailored lapels.

Trousers

Flat-front or pleated, straight or tapered leg.

Shirts

Crisp dress shirts in white or light colors.

Accessories

Silk ties, pocket squares, Oxfords or brogues in leather.

How to Style Tailored Suits

Formal Elegance

Idea: Pair a navy suit with a white dress shirt and silk tie. Opt for polished leather shoes.

Business Professional

Idea: Wear a charcoal suit with a light blue shirt and patterned tie. Ensure a sharp fit.

Modern Twist

Idea: Choose a slim-fit suit in bold colors like burgundy. Style with a patterned shirt and loafers.

Casual Sophistication

Idea: Pair tailored trousers with a knit sweater or polo shirt. Consider loafers or suede shoes.
